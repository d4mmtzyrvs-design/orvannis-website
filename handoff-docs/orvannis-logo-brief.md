# Orvannis — Logo & Identity Creative Brief

A handoff brief for a designer (or an AI logo tool / Canva / Figma). Copy any section directly into your prompt or designer notes.

---

## 1. The brand in one line

**Orvannis** is an all-in-one platform that automates and connects a business's operations — sales, finance, marketing, and workflows — built for small and growing businesses but architected to scale to enterprise.

## 2. Personality

Substantial, intelligent, dependable. Premium but approachable. It should feel like infrastructure you trust — solid and enduring — with a warm, crafted, modern edge. Not flashy, not "startup-cute." Think a platform a CFO and a 5-person team both take seriously.

**Three adjectives to design toward:** *grounded · intelligent · enduring.*

## 3. The concept (story the design should express)

The copper-and-stone palette carries the idea:

- **Copper = the conductive, intelligent layer.** Copper conducts — it's the connective material. It represents Orvannis linking your operations and carrying intelligence through them. Warm, crafted, premium.
- **Stone = the foundation.** Solid, dependable, built to last. It represents the stable backbone your business runs on.

Together: *an intelligent system you can build your business on.* Conductivity + foundation.

## 4. Logo type

**Lead with a wordmark** (the name set in a refined typeface), optionally paired with a small **monogram "O"** mark that can stand alone as an app icon / social avatar. This is the modern standard for B2B platforms — scalable, legible at any size, ages well. No literal illustrations or mascots.

## 5. Mark / symbol concepts (for the standalone "O")

In priority order:

1. **The continuous-loop "O"** — the letter formed by a single unbroken line that loops back on itself, suggesting a cycle that runs on its own (automation, always-on). Elegant in copper. *Primary recommendation.*
2. **The keystone "O"** — an "O" with a subtle architectural arch/keystone motif cut into it, evoking stone, structure, and "the piece that holds everything together."
3. **Concentric rings "O"** — two or three nested rings (layers of operations / orbit), the innermost in copper. Clean and systemic.
4. **Seal/stamp "O"** — a copper ring with the impression of being stamped into stone — crafted, trusted, premium.

Pick one direction; don't combine. Keep it geometric, balanced, and recognizable at 16px.

## 6. Color palette (copper & stone)

Primary accent — **Copper**
- Copper (primary): `#B87333`
- Polished copper (highlight/gradient top): `#CD8B52`
- Antique copper (gradient base / hover): `#9C5A2C`

Neutrals — **Stone**
- Basalt (primary dark / wordmark text): `#33302B`
- Warm stone (secondary text, lines): `#8A8178`
- Limestone (surfaces, cards): `#E7E1D8`
- Paper (background): `#F5F2EC`

Usage: copper is the *accent*, not the whole logo — most of the mark/wordmark should sit in basalt or stone, with copper as the warm highlight (e.g., the loop of the "O", or a single accent letter). On dark backgrounds, flip the wordmark to limestone/paper with the copper accent retained. Avoid pure black (`#000`) and pure white — use basalt and paper instead for the warmer, crafted feel.

## 7. Typography direction

- **Wordmark:** a refined, slightly architectural sans with good weight and even proportions. Reference feel: Söhne, Neue Haas Grotesk, GT America, or (free) Inter / Geist. Set in medium or semibold, generous letter-spacing, lowercase or small-caps for a modern look — *orvannis*.
- *Optional premium alternative:* a contemporary transitional serif (Tiempos, Canela, GT Sera) if you want more gravitas and "built to last." Pick sans **or** serif, not both.
- **Supporting/UI font:** a clean neutral sans (Inter, Söhne, or system UI) for product and web.

## 8. Do / Don't

**Do:**
- Keep it clean, confident, and legible at tiny sizes.
- Make the standalone "O" work as a square app icon.
- Use copper sparingly as a warm accent against stone neutrals.
- Design in vector so it scales infinitely.

**Don't:**
- No gears, robots, circuit boards, or "AI brain" clichés.
- No literal ships/anchors (the maritime idea is set aside for this copper/stone direction).
- No gradients-everywhere; one subtle copper gradient max.
- No more than two fonts. No drop shadows or bevels.

## 9. Deliverables to request (non-negotiable list)

- **Vector source files:** `.SVG` and `.AI` (or `.EPS`) — required so it scales without blurring.
- **PNGs (transparent background):** full-color, all-basalt, all-paper/white versions.
- **Lockups:** horizontal wordmark + mark, stacked version, and the standalone "O" icon.
- **Square avatar** (for social) and **favicon** (16/32px) versions.
- A short **one-page style sheet:** the hex codes above, the font names, and clear-space/minimum-size rules.

## 10. Where this goes

App icon, website header, social avatars, email signature, decks, and eventually product UI — so it must read clearly from a 16px favicon up to a billboard.
