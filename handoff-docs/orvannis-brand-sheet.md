# Orvannis — Brand Sheet

The single source of truth for the Orvannis brand. Keep name, voice, logo, and color consistent everywhere — that consistency is what makes a brand feel intentional.

---

## 1. What Orvannis is

An all-in-one platform that automates and connects a business's operations — sales, finance, marketing, and workflows. Built for small and growing businesses, architected to scale to enterprise.

**Personality (design and write toward these):** *grounded · intelligent · enduring.*
Substantial and dependable, with a warm, crafted, modern edge. Infrastructure you trust — not flashy, not "startup-cute."

---

## 2. The name story

*Canonical version — use on the About page; trim as needed elsewhere.*

> We didn't want a name that described a feature, because features change. We wanted one that described a feeling: permanence and intelligence working together.
>
> So we made one. *Orvannis* was coined to carry two ideas at once — the stability of something built to last, and the quiet intelligence of a system that connects everything and keeps it moving. We think of it the way we think of copper and stone: copper conducts, linking every part of your business and carrying intelligence through it; stone endures, the dependable foundation everything rests on.
>
> A platform you can build on, that's smart enough to run on its own. That's Orvannis.

**One-liner (footers, tooltips):**
> Orvannis is a name we built, not borrowed — chosen to sound like what we're making: solid enough to build a business on, intelligent enough to run it.

**Rule:** Orvannis is a coined name with an assigned meaning. Never claim it's a real word from Latin, Greek, or any language — "we made it up to mean this" is confident and defensible; a fake etymology isn't.

## 4. Logo

**Type:** a wordmark (the name in a refined typeface) plus an optional standalone **"O" monogram** for app icons and social avatars.

**Primary mark concept:** the "O" formed by a single continuous looping line — a cycle that runs on its own (automation, always-on), with copper as the accent.

**Usage rules:**
- Keep clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "O" on all sides.
- Minimum size: wordmark legible at ~80px wide; the "O" icon must read at 16px (favicon).
- The "O" mark must work as a square avatar and a favicon.
- On light backgrounds: basalt wordmark + copper accent. On dark backgrounds: paper/limestone wordmark + copper accent retained.
- Copper is an *accent*, not the whole logo — most of the mark sits in basalt or stone.

**Don't:** stretch, recolor outside the palette, add shadows/bevels/gradients (one subtle copper gradient max), rotate, or place on busy backgrounds. No gears, robots, circuit boards, "AI brain" imagery, or ships/anchors.

## 5. Color palette — copper & stone

**Concept:** copper conducts (the connective, intelligent layer); stone endures (the dependable foundation).

**Accent — Copper**
| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Copper (primary) | `#B87333` | accent, the "O" loop, links, highlights |
| Polished copper | `#CD8B52` | gradient top, hover states |
| Antique copper | `#9C5A2C` | gradient base, pressed states |

**Neutrals — Stone**
| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Basalt | `#33302B` | primary text, wordmark |
| Warm stone | `#8A8178` | secondary text, lines, icons |
| Limestone | `#E7E1D8` | surfaces, cards, dividers |
| Paper | `#F5F2EC` | backgrounds |

**Rules:** Copper is the warm accent against stone neutrals — use it sparingly for emphasis. Avoid pure black (`#000`) and pure white (`#FFF`); use basalt and paper for the warmer, crafted feel.

## 6. Typography

- **Wordmark / headlines:** a refined, slightly architectural sans — even proportions, medium/semibold weight, generous spacing. Reference: Söhne, Neue Haas Grotesk, GT America, or (free) Inter / Geist. *Optional premium alt:* a contemporary serif (Tiempos, Canela) for more gravitas — choose sans **or** serif, not both.
- **Body / UI:** a clean neutral sans (Inter, Söhne, or system UI).
- **Rule:** no more than two typefaces total across the brand.

## 7. Voice & tone

**Voice:** grounded, intelligent, plain-spoken, confident. We sound like a capable expert who respects your time — clear, warm, never hyped.

**Do:**
- Lead with the benefit, in plain language ("Stop doing the busywork," not "leverage synergistic automation").
- Be specific and concrete. Show, don't boast.
- Be calm and assured — we're infrastructure, not a fad.
- Use short sentences and active voice.

**Don't:**
- No buzzword soup ("revolutionary, AI-powered, next-gen, disruptive").
- No exclamation-point hype or fake urgency.
- No condescension — assume the reader is smart and busy.
- No jargon the customer wouldn't use themselves.

**Quick gut-check:** would a sharp, busy small-business owner nod, or roll their eyes? Write for the nod.

---

## 8. Module naming (for future products)

Use a clean `Orvannis [Word]` pattern so the family scales: e.g. **Orvannis Flow** (workflows), **Orvannis Pay** (finance), **Orvannis Connect** (integrations), **Orvannis Reach** (marketing/sales). Keep module words plain, one syllable or two, and benefit-clear.
